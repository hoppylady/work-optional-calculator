Find the age you can call work optional — based on your savings, contributions, and a few reasonable assumptions.

Imagine a machine that quietly pays your bills every year, forever, whether or not you show up to a job. That's "work optional" — the point where your investments alone can cover your life, so a paycheck becomes a choice instead of a requirement. It doesn't mean you'll never work again: plenty of people who reach this point keep working, just on their own terms — fewer hours, a passion project, or nothing at all. Think of it less as a finish line and more as buying yourself a permission slip to choose.

Why savings rate matters more than income

Here's the part that surprises most people: it's not your paycheck that decides how fast you get here — it's what share of it you save. Income is just the faucet; if spending rises right along with it, the tub never fills. Save 10% of anything and you're in for a multi-decade grind. Save half, and you can cut that timeline by more than two-thirds — using the same 7% growth and 4% withdrawal assumptions you can fine-tune below. Same math, any income level, starting from $0.
